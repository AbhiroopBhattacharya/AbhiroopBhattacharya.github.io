About Me
======
My research began long before I entered a PhD program.

As a data scientist in banking, I spent years building models that learned from noisy, incomplete, and constantly changing data. While those systems helped solve practical business problems, they also left me with a deeper question: can machine learning help us discover new knowledge rather than simply make better predictions?

That question eventually led me back to academia.

Today, I am a doctoral researcher at ÉTS Montréal working on multimodal machine learning and AI for Science. I develop learning systems that bring together information from spectroscopy, microscopy, images, time-series signals, and structured scientific measurements to understand and predict material behaviour. My broader goal is to build AI systems that can assist scientific discovery by learning representations that generalize across modalities and domains.

Before academia, I spent eight years at HSBC developing machine learning and statistical models for financial risk. More recently, I have worked on scientific machine learning projects with collaborators in academia and industry, including A*STAR Singapore and Hitachi Energy. My research interests span multimodal learning, geometric deep learning, representation learning, and foundation models for scientific applications.
